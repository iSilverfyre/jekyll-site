<p>Started a new teardown today, a Hitachi Projector Model CP-X2521WN that I got a few months back.  Was fun, not overly challenging to do, lots of neat colored glass and lens were found.  Of course metal as always is going off for recycling to my local yard, plastic with standard recycling and boards will be depopulated and sold for scrap as well.</p>

<hr style="border: 2px solid;">

<p>To start off I removed the screws from the bottom along the border (fig. 1) and a few of the screws off the rear connections panel (fig. 2).  This allowed me to flip it back to right side up and lift the top	straight off (fig. 3).</p>

<p>From there I removed the screws that held down the main board and took that off (fig. 6).  It will go into my bucket off boards that I need to depopulate the components off of at a later date. With the main board out of the way I moved on to one of the 2 cooling systems.  On the bottom left I removed the large black shielding and the fans that fit under neat below the lamp housing (fig. 10).  This as well exposed another smaller control board, in this case what I believe is the main power distribution board (fig. 6).</p>
